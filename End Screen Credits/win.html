Congratulations on Escaping the Mansion

You push open the final door, the heavy wood groaning under your weight.
A rush of cold night air washes over you, sending a shiver down your
spine. You're outside. The moon hangs low in the sky, casting its pale
glow over the mansion's overgrown garden. The path ahead is barely
visible through the mist, but you don't care, you just run.
Behind you, the mansion looms, its dark windows like empty eyes
watching you go. For a moment, you expect to hear the thing's footsteps,
feel its breath on your neck. But there's only silence.
Then, as you cross the threshold of the rusted iron gates,
the mansion lets out a deep, groaning sigh. The walls tremble.
The very air seems to crackle. And in the blink of an eye,
it's gone? Nothing remains but an empty clearing and
the lingering chill of something that should not have been.
You're free. But as you stand there, catching your breath,
a thought creeps into your mind. Was it ever real?
And then, somewhere in the distance.
